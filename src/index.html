It is hard to feel good about yourself, to recover from addictions, to find a job, or participate in society if you do not have means to basic dignity. The starting line for the over 3,000 people experiencing housing insecurity in Calgary can seem unfairly set back from the majority. The problem NFA strives to address is food insecurity and how the simple things make the largest impact in repairing the lives of our most vulnerable populations. Homelessness is hard to miss in a large city. What is more unfortunate is that due to the indiscrete nature of food poverty this issue goes largely unaddressed by social agencies.
